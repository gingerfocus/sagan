#+title: North West Native Culture

Evan Stokdyk
Northwest Coast Essay

![[file:native-chilkat-robe.png.org][center|300]]

Chilkat robes offer a unique way to express family heritage. These worn items, made of goat wool and cedar bark are important to north west coast culture. They are the most accurate representation of a community because they require one to make.

When making a Chilkat robe, first a board is painted with a design and then it is weaved. This two halves are done by males and femals respectively. In addition, the gathering of materials is a separate responsibly furthering the breadth of responsibility. The robes have crests for a given family *moiety* on them, this particular robe has a bear on it. These forms are constructed with various *ovoids*, whose rounded nature would have been impossible on previous forms of weaving. Overall, when the chief wears this item they "became one with the lineage's crest".[fn:1]

Chilkat robes are produced by many people and thus are impacted by many. They also are explicitly created to represent people so it reflects the culture around it.

[fn:1] Aldona Jonaitis, /Art of the Northwest Coast/ (University of Washington Press, 2006), 139.
