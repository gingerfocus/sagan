#+title: Oceania Essay

Oceania Essay

by Evan Stokdyk

![[file:asmat-sheild.png.org][asmat-sheild.png]]
/War shield; Asmat, West Papua; wood, pigment; 175.5 cm H x 50 cm W x 2.60 cm/

--------------

This item is an Asmat war shield created out of wood created in the pacific
islands likely during the mid 20th century. It is both carved and painted to
construct a design of someone lost by the bearer in the abstract. However, it is
only designed to only be understood by the bearer and intimidating to the rest.

Firstly, its relation to the living can also be seen through its material,
wood, which is often associated with humanness. Shields are created for use in
feasts, the warriors go to war to avenge the shield's namesake[1]. The first
purpose is to represent, then its meaning changes showing how it is
[[id:2089CB48-FDED-4183-8541-4AD3100DA0A4][polyvalent]].

When taken to war it is a display piece not a weapon, it is intended to be
visually striking, represents tension and /unbalance/ and signifies what the
bearded fight for. Its meaning shifts with the experience the bearer has.
Therefore, they are the only true authority on it which it means. These shields
are often taken to death; the bearer of the shield transitions to someone else
who may burn it or attempt to finish the shield's vengeance. This represents the
cycles of /life and death/ but also how the meaning can then be interpreted by
the new owner who decides if its journey is not over.

Overall, Asmat shields are complex items which change in value and meaning.
They begin as objects of celebration before becoming objects of fear. Finally,
they can either become objects on commemoration or, alternatively fear again to
show they /cycles/ of warfare.

[1]: Nicholas Thomas, /Oceanic Art/ (London: Thames & Hudson, 2018), 86.
