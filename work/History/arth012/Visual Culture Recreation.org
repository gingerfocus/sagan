​# Analysis

Kapa, also known as tapa, is a bark-cloth that has been repeatedly beaten and woven together to create a type of quilt. The fabric is then dyed so that it can be used to create different patterns which are usually symmetrical. The process that made it placed a large value on the community. Not only did the gathering of materials take collaboration, but many members would all be working on different kapa. In fact, the process of hammering the bark cloth was loud and when done by many people, they would hammer in a rhythm to create a song. Finally, the objects had polyvalent value that would change over time as both the creators and the wearers would interact with it. This particular cloth is red and gold which would have been reserved for royalty[1].

My recreation is the carpet in my dorm. This is reflective of many aspects of what gives kapa cultural significance. Firstly, there is a communal relationship to it as it resides within my dorm and is a shared commodity. Secondly, the beating sounds relate to the music that is played within the dorm. It acts as a place to tap one's feet along with music similar to beating it in tune. Finally, as it has been there all year. My relationship with it has changed throughout the year just like kapa has shifting meaning. However, the cloth does not share the same color scheme as the original as I am not royalty but the pattern is a symmetrical one like the original. Overall, this recreation represents the original visually and materially with its construction, but also culturally in the patterns it is used and interacted with.
