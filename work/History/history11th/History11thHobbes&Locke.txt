The Real Hobbes and Locke

How would you articulate Hobbes’ justification of his own violation of English law and do you see any problems/contradictions in his reasoning?
	Hobbes' justification for the illegal settlement of the Chesapeake was that he believed that the world existed in a state of war so the breaking of laws for your own best interest is natural practice. If every man is against every man you should act in a way that puts you in the best position; Hobbes is an investor in the Virginia Company he stands to gain money by colonizing the Chesapeake. However, a contradiction he makes to his philosophy is that as an investor of the Virginia Company which means that he trusts that someone, who is bound to a state of war, will not betray him and his money. Furthermore, he claims in The Leviathan that the American Indians are "brutish" despite the ongoing anarchy present in the colony he wrote this in.

Can you think of any possible argument Locke could have made to square this contradiction given Locke continued to support slavery in Carolina even after writing Two Treatises?
	One argument he could make was that he simply did not agree with the morals he outlined in the Two Treatises of Government. He published it anonymously showing that he did not want these ideas to be tied to his name. However, it is also very likely that he agreed with his writing to an extent but as a politician he wanted to reward the people who vote for him to keep their support. Furthermore, he may have believed that passing laws in contradiction to his beliefs to continue making decisions was the right choice. Overall, both of these possibilities raise a concern of moral low ground, showing that he may have not been the same person he illustrates in the Two Treatises of Government.
