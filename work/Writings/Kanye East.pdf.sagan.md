Citizen Kane Response

Writter: Dec 8, 2022

For: Baril Cinima Studies

One way the movie drew a strong reaction was my general dislike for the
characters. I have spoken on this before but the movies that have the most
unlikeable characters tend to be the most interesting. They are not enjoyable
but that is independent of interest. The difference here is that it felt more
difficult to separate the fiction of the movie from reality and I found myself
getting mad at someone who didn’t exist. Although this movie was made as an
allusion to someone, I had never heard of that person so being that convincing
is quite a feat. The way the movie was able to do this was from the long,
always in focus shots. That is the way real life is viewed and in some scenes
the camera takes the role of someone you could imagine as yourself. However,
there are many scenes where the camera is placed somewhere (like on the floor)
where you would not be putting the viewer in a more observational role for that
scene. In this way, by controlling which scenes the viewer feels like a
participant and which they feel like an observer the movie is able to control
that the watcher learns and focuses on what the director wants. The reading
informed me about the outsized impact of the photographer in the movie which
provides justification for what I observed. Although I find the lack of sharing
a title page strange, it helps to illustrate just how crucial using the camera
to control the viewer experience was to this movie.
