Evan Stokdyk 5/01/2024 Essay 1 Engl 2a - Leither The Big Game The house always wins. It's the first, and a sensible gambler's only, rule of gambling. It's also the reason gambling is a business at all, no money is given away. Neither of these things could be seen as anything near controversial. However, sports betting, parleys or drafting, three names for alternatives to gambling, promise to bring a different skill based alternative to this. University is a time in a person's life where they learn additional skills and habits to perform their career. In addition, college students often have less excess money. This leads to an opportunity for brands to offer discounts to students to use their product in the hope that they will continue to use it later in life. Gambling, which promises to systematically liberate a person from their money, is a bad habit to develop early in someone's life at full price. The rising popularity of sports betting, not just as entertainment but often seen as a job, is a concern for the wellbeing of university life. Stacie Stern, a Government Affairs Director at FanDuel, argues wagering has a social impact on students, however these peer pressures are already present and thus it is the responsibility for a good student to know how to navigate them. Additionally, gambling can have good side effects on its surroundings. The profits from healthy gambling can be used to prevent misuse of gambling. Sports betting can provide universities with new revenue sources allowing them to expand programs that help students. In addition, through having betting controlled by Stokdyk 2 regulated organizations it can help prevent underage gambling. Gambling can have horrible effects on people's lives but, sports betting is inevitable even when illegal but when it's controlled, it can provide economic benefits that outweigh and can be used to fund solutions to the problems it creates. However, the reality of campuses sponsored by bookies differs from this vision. Since the introduction of wagers to school campuses, many of the supposed good changes have yet to come. In addition, despite the clever name change and arguments about skill, sports betting is still gambling. Changing the name can get rid of the connotations, but not the reality that wagers are systematically designed to generate profit for the bookmakers long term. Gambling not only affects the lives of individuals but those around them, as well the teams they are supposed to support. The claim that betting can bring benefits is flawed in that it views the world from the economic perspective of only the universities, blind to the financial well being of students and ignoring non-monetary damages such as social. Finally, it creates perverse incentives for schools as it provides an easier route to profit than investing in fair athletics. Although sports betting claims to enrich student life, legal gambling on college sports leads to an overall worse experience for students. Sports betting attempts to separate itself from gambling in its marketing, however in reality it is just the same shit, different day. The psychological effects of fantasy betting greatly resemble gambling as the processes are different but end in the same result. For example, “fantasy sports players exhibit behaviors associated with addiction and problem gambling, with negative outcomes associated with addiction and problem gambling, with negative outcomes at both work and home” (Houghton, p.334). The effects of gambling can affect other parts of people's lives, not just monetary meaning it can be hard to fix. The bad sides of gambling are Stokdyk 3 well documented however another misconception of sports gambling is that it results in losing less money or even gaining money. Wagers can still result in the same degenerative habits as regular gambling and often just as much money is at stake for the loser. People on social internet platforms such as Youtube and Tiktok will claim that they can make money with parleys. One such person, Caan Berry Pro Trader, has a 8 minute video titled Under/Over Football Betting Strategy to Win Repeatedly. It begins with 2 and a half minutes of emphasizing the ease and quantity of money that can be made. This is meant to drive engagement as people fear that they are missing out. The strategy he explains is even somewhat viable (given that you assume that the market can accurately price the randomness of the game and injury). However, because sports betting is a zero sum game, and often a negative sum game when excluding the bookie, any money made needs to be lost by someone else. The requirement for someone else to lose shows a flawed system, even so, assuming there is money to be made, an aspiring better must outperform not just the bookkeeper but also others doing the same, machines set to capitalize on tiny arbitrage opportunities and cheaters who can fix games. There is money that could be made with sports betting, just as there is money that could be made by buying a scratcher and putting your money on red, there's risk, that's what it means to gamble. This mindset disproportionately affects young adults who see parleys as an opportunity compared to current jobs. This can partly be attributed to the fact that there has been a shift in perspective as “U.S. media coverage increasingly framed casino gambling as mainstream entertainment” (Petrotta). This shift allowed people who lack previous experience with proximity to gambling addiction, often young people, to start gaming casually. Sports betting is very profitable at the expense of others and struggles to bring any auxiliary benefit to those who partake in it. Gambling promises to help students through Stokdyk 4 increased school funding and regulation, however this assumes that student welfare can be purchased. Often, the damages from gambling culture are non-reversible and so its adoption is less like a trade but rather a compromise that includes both large gains and losses. Oftentimes what is lost is student agency to decide what they would like to engage with due to a bombardment of advertisements. For example, “After Louisiana State University signed a similar deal in 2021 with Caesars, the university sent an email encouraging recipients --- including some students who were under 21 and couldn't legally gamble --- to ‘place your first bet (and earn your first bonus)'“ (Betts). To navigate around gambling in a healthy way there must be a choice to engage. To claim to be protecting illegal betting and then openly solicit underage gambling is counter productive. This is the duality that sports gambling takes because when dealing with something addictive you can't remove the idea from someone's head. Finally, legal sports betting creates a conflict of interest at every level for universities. Athletes are put in difficult situations with very little barging power due to weak financial positions. Schools are rewarded for investing in what gives them the most money, advertisements, rather than their students. College athletes are unpaid and are not held to the same scrutiny as professionals. It can be harder for them to say no to schemes such as point-shaving where they intentionally perform worse in a game to make someone else money. This is hard to detect but evidence suggests that “around 1 percent of all [college basketball] games involve gambling related corruption” (Wolfers, p.283). This figure is from 2006 when there was significantly less money to be made. This corruption is not prevented with a more fair bookmaker as it takes advantage of the reality of university life. In addition, these schemes can also apply in reverse where an athlete is paid to over perform through the use of doping. This not only destroyed the bet's integrity but also the sports. Universities are interested in generating Stokdyk 5 revenue from their popular sports to keep them running and provide money for the school. Traditionally, this was done through investing in talent and gaining sponsorships and tv time. However, the advertisement money brought by betting companies is often greater than other options. This means schools are incentivised to invest ease of gambling for their students rather than a better sports team. In addition, many of the things that influence a player's fair play can also be a way the college teams can fix matches. Overall, the presence of the change to make money corrupts college athletics. Sports betting promised to bring a change to how gambling was perceived, it managed to do that but without changing the harm it caused, roping thousands of people into degenerative wagers. Allowing its promotion within universities will only accelerate the vice it has over our current culture. Gambling provides no value other than an escape to the disillusioned, and false hope to the doomed. Sports betting is the same gambling in that it is a zero sum game, the house always wins, so everyone else must lose. With the traditional ideal of owning a home becoming a more distant goal for most people, it is no wonder that despite knowing that gambling has poor odds, many young adults believe it is their chance at reaching a more respectable life.
