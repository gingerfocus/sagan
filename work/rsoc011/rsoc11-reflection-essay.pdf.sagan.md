1040 Movie Reflection  

The 1040 documentary offers a purely informative movie experience to give the
audience facts and let them discover their own conclusion. It does this first
by describing the history of christianity in each region briefly and then
focusing on how youth culture challenges these norms. One positive aspect of
the film is its regional focus. Each section spends ample time to show events,
consequences and people who embody the culture there. I fervently enjoyed the
way in which they let the human experiences of each person shine on through by
using the mediums of video. Bringing in experts or other qualified people and
seeing them communicate directly was a great way to see their passion shine
through. However, one limitation of the film is that the 1040 region that the
film aims to cover is massive and contains many people. It represents a large
challenge to summarize it in any reasonable amount of time which results in
some parts not being covered. In addition, there is a problem of false
equivalence in which each region is presented as if they have a similar
christian movement when in reality they differ in size. This film enlightened
me to a few things, one of them being the larger christian movement in China. I
was particularly surprised by some of the explanations of the etymology of
characters and how they could be viewed in a christian light. I was also
pleasantly surprised by the way evangelicalism worked in Indonesia, which has
the highest muslims population in the world. There, priests only preach the
gospel to muslims and not try to convert them, they instead ask what problems
they have and then try to fix them. One through line I noticed in most regions
was the commonality of christian rock. This may represent a trend or may be a
bias of this research. Alternatively, the film has a message of unity
regardless of practice or faith which I find very surprising and counter to my
experience with religions. Overall, the films shows a positive feature towards
faith in Asia which is based on the teachings of the bible based in
philanthropy separated from traditional church structures.
